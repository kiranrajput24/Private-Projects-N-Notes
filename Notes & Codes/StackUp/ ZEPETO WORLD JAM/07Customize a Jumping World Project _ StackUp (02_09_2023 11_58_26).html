Introduction
In this last quest, you will create your first official jumping World using the features learned from the previous quests. In preparation for the ZEPETO WORLD JAM event, this quest will also guide you on how to make a World Console where you will register your jumping World and can submit your World for a review by the ZEPETO team.
For technical help on the StackUp platform & quest-related questions, join our Discord, head to the #quest-help channel and quote (C49Q7) when asking your question.
Learning Outcomes
By the end of this quest, you will have:
  • Set up the ZEPETO World Console
  • Created and customized a jumping World
  • Implemented features learned in previous quests to your World
  • Registered your project in the World Console
Deliverable
This quest has three deliverables.
  1. A screenshot of your jumping World project. Follow instructions in Step 2 for more details.
  2. A test URL link of your registered file in the World Console. Follow instructions in Step 3 for more details.
  3. A screenshot of your registered file in the World Console. Follow instructions in Step 3 for more details.

Step 2: Create a Jumping World
With your World console set up for the new project, you can build your official complete World - a simple jumping World where you can collect items along the obstacle course. 
Follow these steps in order to create your jumping World with the necessary requirements:
  1. Design a Map of your World on Build it with at least 15 Objects for players to jump and move around on. You can build a Map with any objects to make it as interactive as you want for players.
    1. For Linux users - Create a Map of your World directly in Unity with at least 15 Objects (make sure to create a Plane first as your ground base for your Map)
  2. In the Jumping World project, implement the features you have learned in previous quests - character controller, movement of GameObjects, the leaderboard, and item collection
    1. Movement of GameObjects - have objects both rotate and move around

Refer back to the previous quests to complete the requirements. Feel free to check out this link to create your World and find the scripts for rotating and moving an object.

Click Play to test your jumping World and ensure that you have fulfilled the requirements. Take a screenshot of your World with your player clearly visible through the Scene point of view, just like what you see in “Expected Output”. 

Your screenshot should show:
  • Your full screen, including your taskbar (for Windows) / dock (for MacOS)
  • Your player and the entire layout of your World through the Scene POV
  • All of the GameObjects under the Hierarchy window
  • All of the scripts and Scene in the Assets folder
  • Make sure that all parts visible in ‘Expected Output’ are also visible in your screenshot(s)!

Rename your screenshot “C49_Q7_yourStackUpName_1.png”, where yourStackUpName is your StackUp profile name.
